# Vector and Speech Recognition {#vector-and-speech-recognition .title}

[](javascript:window.print() "Print this article"){.printArticle
aria-label="Print this Article"}

[Learn below how to give a Voice Command to Vector, how to ask him a
question and how to communicate with him in other ways. Vector is
currently monolingual and can only understand
English.]{style="font-weight: 400;"}

## [Vector detects]{style="font-weight: 400;"} {#h_111212124131538839874145}

-   [Vector's 4 microphone array uses beamforming to detect directional
    sound and background sound, though he does filter out some
    background sound]{style="font-weight: 400;"}
-   [He may react differently depending on if he is awake or asleep,
    whether you use a "trigger word" to get his attention, or if he is
    startled]{style="font-weight: 400;"}
-   [For best results, always put Vector down on a hard, flat surface
    (and not in his Space) before speaking to
    him]{style="font-weight: 400;"}

## [Voice Commands]{style="font-weight: 400;"}

[Get Vector's attention to give him a Voice Command - telling him to do
something:]{style="font-weight: 400;"}

-   [Say "Hey Vector!" This is his "Wake
    Word"]{style="font-weight: 400;"}
-   [Wait for his Back lights to turn blue and he will "ding" his
    earcon. This confirms you have his attention and he is ready to
    accept a Voice Command]{style="font-weight: 400;"}
-   [Give Vector a command e.g. "Come
    here!"![](//d33v4339jhl8k0.cloudfront.net/docs/assets/5e3f0b1e2c7d3a7e9ae777f5/images/5e7d764004286364bc975abe/img-25345-1585280575-1382936130.gif)]{style="font-weight: 400;"}

[]{style="font-weight: 400;"}

[Give Vector a "Utilities" Voice Command e.g. asking what time it is,
asking what the weather is, setting a timer:]{style="font-weight: 400;"}

-   [Utilities use local preferences from your device settings and
    information you input into the app during setup such as location and
    time zone]{style="font-weight: 400;"}
-   [You still need to get Vector's attention with the Wake Word "Hey
    Vector" and wait for the ding and his Back Lights to turn
    blue]{style="font-weight: 400;"}
-   [Say, "Hey Vector, what's the weather?" Vector's answer is based on
    the location in your app settings]{style="font-weight: 400;"}
-   [Note: you can also ask Vector the weather in Vector Q&A (see below)
    but you will need to specify a location for him to look up
     ]{style="font-weight: 400;"}

[To learn more about what Commands you can give, take a look at "Things
to Try" in the Vector companion app]{style="font-weight: 400;"}

[If you encounter any issues using Voice Commands, see
]{style="font-weight: 400;"}[this
FAQ](//support.digitaldreamlabs.com/article/99-vector-does-not-understand-me-troubleshooting-speech-recognition)[
for tips and troubleshooting]{style="font-weight: 400;"}

## [Vector Q&A: Ask Vector a question]{style="font-weight: 400;"} {#h_503377977321538839886944}

[Asking Vector a question uses a specific provider, available in
American English only.]{style="font-weight: 400;"}

![](//d33v4339jhl8k0.cloudfront.net/docs/assets/5e3f0b1e2c7d3a7e9ae777f5/images/5e7d76402c7d3a7e9ae9e1ba/img-25345-1585280575-936313858.gif)

[]{style="font-weight: 400;"}

1.  [Get Vector's attention using one of the following two
    triggers:]{style="font-weight: 400;"}

2.  1.  ["Hey Vector, I have a question"]{style="font-weight: 400;"}
    2.  ["Hey Vector, look up a question for
        me"]{style="font-weight: 400;"}

3.  [Vector will show he understood by displaying the above
    icon]{style="font-weight: 400;"}

4.  [When he is ready to be asked, he will tell you,
    "Ready"]{style="font-weight: 400;"}

5.  [Ask Vector a question -- the more specific you are, the more likely
    he is to answer your question e.g. "What will the weather be like in
    London [**on Saturday**]{.wysiwyg-color-blue} [**in degrees
    celsius**]{.wysiwyg-color-green120}"]{style="font-weight: 400;"}

6.  [If the question has multiple answers, he may give the answer that
    is first alphabetically]{style="font-weight: 400;"}

7.  [If Vector didn't detect or understand your question you will have
    to start again with "Hey Vector, I have  a question"
    ]{style="font-weight: 400;"}

[Take a look at Vector Q&A in "Things to Try" in the Vector companion
app to learn more about it.]{style="font-weight: 400;"}

[If you encounter any issues using Vector Q&A, see
]{style="font-weight: 400;"}[this
FAQ](//support.digitaldreamlabs.com/article/99-vector-does-not-understand-me-troubleshooting-speech-recognition)[
for tips and troubleshooting.]{style="font-weight: 400;"}

## [Turning off voice detection]{style="font-weight: 400;"}  {#h_630555305361538839895208}

-   You can mute or unmute Vector's microphone array so that he can't
    hear any voice commands by double-clicking his Back Button - he must
    be off his Charger. Vector will display a "Mic Mute" icon on his
    face **for a brief moment.**
-   While Vector's mic is muted, his Back Lights are red. Vector will
    stay muted until you unmute him.
-   To unmute Vector's mic, just double tap his Back button again while
    he's off his Charger You can tell he's unmuted, you will see a "Mic
    Unmute" icon on his face.
-   For more information on what Vector's Back Lights mean, please see
    [this
    FAQ](//support.digitaldreamlabs.com/article/44-what-do-vectors-back-lights-mean)
-   [For further information on Vector and privacy, please refer to
    ]{style="font-weight: 400;"}[this
    FAQ](//support.digitaldreamlabs.com/article/105-vector-security-privacy-faqs)
