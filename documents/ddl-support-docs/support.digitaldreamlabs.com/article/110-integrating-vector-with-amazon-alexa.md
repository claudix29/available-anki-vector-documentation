## [Vector now includes Alexa!]{style="font-weight: 400;"} {#h_5747654153481545042555942}

[Alexa allows you to use your voice to hear the news, check weather,
control your smart home, and more. All you have to do is ask
Alexa.]{style="font-weight: 400;"}
:::

------------------------------------------------------------------------

::: sections
## [Vector stays Vector ]{style="font-weight: 400;"} {#h_6267613914341545042561086}

[Enabling Alexa is completely optional, and Alexa isn't enabled by
default. At his core, Vector stays Vector. ]{style="font-weight: 400;"}

[Even when you enable Alexa, Vector maintains all of his abilities and
his personality. Any time you say "Hey Vector" before giving Vector a
voice command, Vector is always the one to carry it
out.]{style="font-weight: 400;"}

[You'll still say "Hey Vector" to have Vector do things like "Take a
Picture", "Do a trick", or "Come here". Alexa won't move Vector around,
make him do a trick, or let him take a picture of
you.]{style="font-weight: 400;"}
:::

------------------------------------------------------------------------

::: sections
## [Vector won't do everything Alexa can do]{style="font-weight: 400;"} {#h_4200495265791545042568046}

[Vector does not support the following:]{style="font-weight: 400;"}

-   [Communicating with other people with features like Drop In,
    Calling, and Messaging]{style="font-weight: 400;"}
-   [Playing songs and podcasts from streaming services like Amazon
    Prime Music, Spotify, Pandora, SiriusXM]{style="font-weight: 400;"}
-   [Reading books out loud through Kindle or
    Audible]{style="font-weight: 400;"}
:::

## [Enabling Alexa]{style="font-weight: 400;"} {#h_1613581896551545042573423}

[To enable Alexa on Vector, you will need an Amazon account and use the
Vector Robot app:]{style="font-weight: 400;"}

-   [Launch the Vector Robot app and connect to your
    Vector]{style="font-weight: 400;"}
-   [From the Home screen, click Amazon Alexa under THINGS TO
    TRY]{style="font-weight: 400;"}
-   [Click the "SIGN IN WITH AMAZON" button]{style="font-weight: 400;"}
-   [At the Amazon Sign in, either]{style="font-weight: 400;"}

1.  [Click the "Create a new Amazon account" button if you do not
    already have one or]{style="font-weight: 400;"}
2.  [Enter your Amazon account credentials and click the "Sign in"
    button]{style="font-weight: 400;"}

[Once you are logged in with your account, register Your Device (Vector)
by typing in the 6-digit code displayed on Vector's face and clicking
the "Continue" button]{style="font-weight: 400;"}

[![alexa-code.png](//d33v4339jhl8k0.cloudfront.net/docs/assets/5e3f0b1e2c7d3a7e9ae777f5/images/5e7d76df2c7d3a7e9ae9e32d/img-4276-1585280572-1347361886.png){width="250"
height="140"}]{style="font-weight: 400;"}

[Your Vector is now registered with Amazon
Alexa.]{style="font-weight: 400;"}

[![alexa-setup-succeded.png](//d33v4339jhl8k0.cloudfront.net/docs/assets/5e3f0b1e2c7d3a7e9ae777f5/images/5e7d76e004286364bc975c18/img-4276-1585280572-1553148300.png){width="250"
height="140"}]{style="font-weight: 400;"}

 

[When you have enabled an Alexa Skill and it sends a
[**notification**](https://www.amazon.com/gp/help/customer/display.html/?nodeId=202165800),
Vector's Back Light will flash yellow until you clear all of your
notifications. The yellow Alexa notifications override other lights. For
more information on what Vector's Back Lights mean, please see
[**this**](https://support.anki.com/hc/articles/360009969334-What-do-Vector-s-Back-Lights-Mean-)
[**article**](https://support.anki.com/hc/articles/360009969334-What-do-Vector-s-Back-Lights-Mean-).]{style="font-weight: 400;"}

[If you do not see the \"Amazon Alexa\" button under \"Things to Try\",
please make sure the Vector app and Robot Operating System are on the
[**latest
version**](https://support.anki.com/hc/articles/360010539153-Vector-Changelog-and-Known-Issues).]{style="font-weight: 400;"}

## [Using Alexa]{style="font-weight: 400;"} {#h_2089991927121545042578638}

[Once activated, use Alexa the same way you do today on any other Alexa
enabled device: say "Alexa", followed by a voice command. You can tell
Alexa to control your lights, set a timer, check the weather, or most
other things you normally use Alexa for.]{style="font-weight: 400;"}

[]{style="font-weight: 400;"}

[ ]{style="font-weight: 400;"}

## [Disabling Alexa]{style="font-weight: 400;"} {#h_4255747249771545042602855}

[There are several ways to disable Vector's Alexa
integration.]{style="font-weight: 400;"}

[ ]{style="font-weight: 400;"}

[You can sign out of your Amazon account using the Vector Robot
companion app. Signing out will turn off Alexa until you sign in with
Amazon again.]{style="font-weight: 400;"}

[ ]{style="font-weight: 400;"}

### [To use the Vector Robot app:]{style="font-weight: 400;"}

-   [Launch the Vector Robot app and connect to your
    Vector]{style="font-weight: 400;"}
-   [From the Home screen, click Amazon Alexa under THINGS TO
    TRY]{style="font-weight: 400;"}
-   [Click the "SIGN OUT OF AMAZON" button]{style="font-weight: 400;"} 

### [To disable Alexa with Vector himself: ]{style="font-weight: 400;"}

-   [Say "Hey Vector", "Disable Alexa"]{style="font-weight: 400;"}
-   [You can also remove Vector from your Amazon account through the
    Amazon Alexa app or the Amazon's
    ]{style="font-weight: 400;"}**[Manage Your Content and Devices
    site](https://www.amazon.com/mycd)**
