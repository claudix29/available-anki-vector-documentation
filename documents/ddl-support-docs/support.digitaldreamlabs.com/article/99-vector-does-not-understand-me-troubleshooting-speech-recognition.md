# Vector does not understand me: Troubleshooting Speech Recognition {#vector-does-not-understand-me-troubleshooting-speech-recognition .title}

[](javascript:window.print() "Print this article"){.printArticle
aria-label="Print this Article"}

[If you are having trouble getting Vector's attention or getting him to
react to Commands or Questions, learn some tips and tricks below so
Vector understands! Also learn how to rule out any issues with Vector's
microphones and Digital Dream Labs Cloud
Services.]{style="font-weight: 400;"}

## [Tips]{style="font-weight: 400;"} {#h_28786684591538840362648}

-   [Make sure you have selected US, GB or AU English based on your
    accent, not your location]{style="font-weight: 400;"}
-   [Familiarize yourself with the Vector companion app where you can
    find a list of Voice Commands and tips on how to construct
    them]{style="font-weight: 400;"}
-   [Make sure Vector is fully charged - he will not accept any Voice
    Commands when he is low on battery and on his way back to the
    charger]{style="font-weight: 400;"}
-   [Press Vector's Back Button once to bypass using the Wake Word ("Hey
    Vector") if Vector is not reacting to this. Continue by giving a
    Voice Command]{style="font-weight: 400;"}
-   [Remember to wait for the earcon "ding" and the blue Back Light
    before giving a Voice Command]{style="font-weight: 400;"}
-   [Vector will let you know if he cannot recognize or perform the
    command you give him. In this instance, try the following
    troubleshooting]{style="font-weight: 400;"}
:::

------------------------------------------------------------------------

::: sections
## [Troubleshooting Steps]{style="font-weight: 400;"} {#h_674331044191538840369641}

-   [Place Vector on a hard, flat surface that is not his Space -- he
    may not perform all Voice Commands if he is being
    held]{style="font-weight: 400;"}

-   [Similar to a person, Vector finds it more difficult to understand
    when there is background noise]{style="font-weight: 400;"}

-   -   [Speak clearly, loudly and be close (but not too close...) to
        Vector when you speak to him]{style="font-weight: 400;"}
    -   [If Vector is moving or making noise, wait for him to stop
        before speaking to him. You can also pet him so he focuses and
        stops moving]{style="font-weight: 400;"}

-   [Speak at a normal speed, not any slower than you usually would.
    This would be like with speaking louder to someone who does not
    understand your language... it does not help them
    understand]{style="font-weight: 400;"}

-   [Enunciate -- just like Customer Care reps over the phone, Vector
    really needs to hear those Ps&Bs, your Fs&Ss, your Ts&Ds, your
    M&Ns]{style="font-weight: 400;"}

-   [Remember that you are not having a conversation -- Vector will not
    understand a Voice Command if it is mixed into a sentence e.g. "Come
    here!" is a Voice Command while "Oh Vector, you're so cute, come
    here, I want to pet you" is not]{style="font-weight: 400;"}

-   [Make sure you only ask him one thing at a time. He will not
    understand compound commands:]{style="font-weight: 400;"}

-   -   [First: Hey Vector \[wait\] Come
        here!]{style="font-weight: 400;"}
    -   [Then: Hey Vector \[wait\] Give me a fist
        bump!]{style="font-weight: 400;"}
    -   [Not: Hey Vector \[wait\] Come here and give me a fist
        bump!]{style="font-weight: 400;"}
:::

------------------------------------------------------------------------

::: sections
## [Using the Customer Care Info Screen (CCIS) to test the microphones ]{style="font-weight: 400;"} {#h_221283337281538840374526}

1.  [Place Vector on his charger and plug the charger
    in]{style="font-weight: 400;"}
2.  [\[If Vector is booting up, wait for the "V" to
    finish\]]{style="font-weight: 400;"}
3.  [Double click Vector's Back Button ]{style="font-weight: 400;"}
4.  [Raise and lower Vector's lift]{style="font-weight: 400;"}
5.  [Lower and raise Vector's head]{style="font-weight: 400;"}
6.  [Press his Back Button 6 times]{style="font-weight: 400;"}
7.  [If the MICS indicate a positive value, the microphones are in
    working order. If MICS indicates a value of "0", please
    ]{style="font-weight: 400;"}contact Customer Care via email at
    support@digitaldreamlabs.com[ for further
    help]{style="font-weight: 400;"}

[If only 1 type of speech recognition (e.g. Voice Command
]{style="font-weight: 400;"}**or**[ Vector Q&A) is not working as
expected, please ]{style="font-weight: 400;"}contact Customer Care
(support@digitaldreamlabs.com)[.]{style="font-weight: 400;"}[ This may
be due to an issue with a Digital Dream Labs Cloud
Service.]{style="font-weight: 400;"}

[If Vector's MICS are working fine and he reacts to bypassing "Hey
Vector" with his Back Button press but you still struggle to use Voice
Commands or Ask Vector, ]{style="font-weight: 400;"}[check your WiFi
connection](//support.digitaldreamlabs.com/article/104-troubleshooting-vectore28099s-connection)[
-- intermittent or poor WiFi quality may affect how well Vector can
recognise speech and process reactions.]{style="font-weight: 400;"}

[If your WiFi connection is also fine, please continue
reading.]{style="font-weight: 400;"}
:::

::: sections
## [Vector supports English]{style="font-weight: 400;"} {#h_853410442331538840382623}

[While the richness and diversity of language is a wonderful thing,
unfortunately neither Vector nor any other devices that use speech
recognition can cover the full spectrum of different English dialects
and accents.]{style="font-weight: 400;"}

[If you have followed the above, you have ruled out a technical defect,
as well as other potential factors that can affect the performance of
Vectors Speech Recognition. As such, it seems that your little robot
generally struggles with your particular intonation, dialect, accent
and/or regionalisms.]{style="font-weight: 400;"}

[We trust that over time, the voice recognition success rate will be
greatly improved.]{style="font-weight: 400;"}
