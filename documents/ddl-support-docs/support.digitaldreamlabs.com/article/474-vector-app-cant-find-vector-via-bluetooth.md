# Vector App Can\'t Find Vector via Bluetooth {#vector-app-cant-find-vector-via-bluetooth .title}

[](javascript:window.print() "Print this article"){.printArticle
aria-label="Print this Article"}

When you receive the error \"Vector App Can\'t Find Vector via
Bluetooth,\" this is usually an easy fix with a few common root causes.
Let\'s explore some ways to resolve this in just a few
seconds!![](//d33v4339jhl8k0.cloudfront.net/docs/assets/5e3f0b1e2c7d3a7e9ae777f5/images/61e7204fd86136157d99afda/file-vqMK5dFgKC.jpg){style="width: 363px;"}

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Causes:

-   Vector is not in Pairing Mode
-   Bluetooth is not enabled on the mobile device
-   The Vector Robot app does not have access to Bluetooth permissions

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Resolutions:

1.  Make sure Vector is in Pairing Mode:\
    1.  Place Vector on his Charger (**Note: The Charger must be plugged
        in and powered according to [these
        requirements](//support.digitaldreamlabs.com/article/117-how-to-charge-vector){target="_blank"}.**)
    2.  Double tap his Back Button (This button is on Vector\'s LED
        strip on his back. The button will make an audible *click* when
        pressed correctly.)
    3.  An icon of a key with 6 pound signs (\"######\") will appear on
        his screen.
    4.  Click \"Reconnect.\"
    5.  When the PIN appears on Vector\'s screen, enter it into the app
        to [continue with
        setup](//support.digitaldreamlabs.com/article/114-video-vector-and-connection-how-to-set-vector-up){target="_blank"}.
2.  Enable Bluetooth on the mobile device:\
    1.  Follow the steps in [Enabling Bluetooth On Your
        Device](//support.digitaldreamlabs.com/article/475-enabling-bluetooth-on-your-device){target="_blank"}
    2.  [](//support.digitaldreamlabs.com/article/475-enabling-bluetooth-on-your-device){target="_blank"}Click
        \"Reconnect.\"
    3.  When the PIN appears on Vector\'s screen, enter it into the app
        to [continue with
        setup](//support.digitaldreamlabs.com/article/114-video-vector-and-connection-how-to-set-vector-up).
3.  Make sure the Vector Robot app has permissions to access Bluetooth
    on your device:
    1.  **Instructions for iPhone**:
        1.  Scroll through your Home screens to find the \"Settings\"
            app, then click on it to enter the Settings menu for your
            device.
        2.  Scroll all the way to the bottom of the Settings menu, and
            find the \"Vector Robot\" app. Click on it to enter the
            app\'s Settings submenu.
        3.  Under \"Allow Vector Robot to Access\", make sure that the
            \"Bluetooth\" and \"Local Network\" sliders are positioned
            to the right and that they appear green. If they are turned
            off, turn them on.
        4.  Retry the connection to Vector.
    2.  **Instructions for Android**:
        1.  Find the Settings app (this will vary by software setup and
            phone manufacturer):
            1.  If you have an App Drawer:
                1.  Locate the App Drawer button at the bottom of the
                    screen.
                2.  Click the App Drawer icon.
                3.  Rotate through App Drawer screens until you find the
                    Settings app.
                4.  Click the Settings icon to enter the Settings app.
            2.  If you do not have an App Drawer:
                1.   Rotate through your Home screens to find the
                    Settings icon.
                2.  Click on the Settings icon to enter the Settings
                    menu.
        2.  Locate the \"Apps\" menu and enable permissions for the
            Vector Robot app:
            1.  Scroll down in the Settings menu until you find
                \"Apps.\" Click to enter the Apps menu.
            2.  Scroll down until you find \"Vector Robot\", or enter
                \"Vector Robot\" into the search if you have a search
                function in this menu.
            3.  Click on the \"Vector Robot\" entry.
            4.  Click \"Permissions.\"
            5.  Click on each entry under the \"Not Allowed\" section
                and enable any permissions that were not allowed at
                install.
                1.  **Note: We use \"Location\" to run Wi-Fi scans as
                    required by the Android permissions system. We do
                    not collect a precise GPS location from the device
                    for any reason.**
                2.  **Note: We use \"Files and Media\" to export photos
                    from Vector\'s camera and save them to your
                    device.**
