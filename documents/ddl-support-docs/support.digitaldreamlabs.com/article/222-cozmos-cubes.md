## What battery does the cube use?

-   [Alkaline N / E90 / LR1 1.5 Volt
    batteries]{style="font-weight: 400;"}
-   [Do not use A23 batteries - these will damage the
    Cube]{style="font-weight: 400;"}
-   [Do not use rechargeable batteries - these will damage the
    Cube]{style="font-weight: 400;"}
:::

------------------------------------------------------------------------

::: sections
## How long does the battery last?

-   [Batteries come pre-installed in Cozmo\'s
    Cube]{style="font-weight: 400;"}

-   [Once the pull-tab is removed, the battery will start to lose
    charge]{style="font-weight: 400;"}

-   [The battery will last 20-40 hours of gameplay depending on how much
    Cozmo interacts with the Cube]{style="font-weight: 400;"}

-   [To test if the battery is working, reset the
    cube:]{style="font-weight: 400;"}

-   -   [unscrew the lid from the cube]{style="font-weight: 400;"}
    -   [wait for 10 seconds]{style="font-weight: 400;"}
    -   [screw the lid back on]{style="font-weight: 400;"}
    -   [the cube should flash red\>green\>blue if the battery is
        fine]{style="font-weight: 400;"}

-   [In Settings \> Cube Status, there will be a
    [red]{.wysiwyg-color-red} Low Battery icon if the battery does not
    have enough power to sync / connect and needs to be replaced. Note:
    the low battery icon does not indicate battery
    voltage]{style="font-weight: 400;"}
:::

------------------------------------------------------------------------

::: sections
## How do I change the battery?

1.  [Remove the cube's screw using a cross head screwdriver and open the
    cube's cover]{style="font-weight: 400;"}
2.  [Remove and ]{style="font-weight: 400;"}[safely
    dispose](//support.digitaldreamlabs.com/article/129-product-disposal-information)[
    of the old battery]{style="font-weight: 400;"}
3.  [Insert a new battery, positive end up]{style="font-weight: 400;"}
4.  [Replace the cover and screw the cover back down (not too tightly
    which can damage the cube)]{style="font-weight: 400;"}

[![Battery_Diagram.png](//d33v4339jhl8k0.cloudfront.net/docs/assets/5e3f0b1e2c7d3a7e9ae777f5/images/5e7d76e704286364bc975c21/img-25345-1585280584-1033400553.png)]{style="font-weight: 400;"}
:::

::: sections
## How does Cozmo interact with his cubes?

Cozmo interacts with his cubes on two levels: 

-   **Visually:** Cozmo can tell the difference between each cube by its
    markings. He can recognize the cube even without a battery

![](//d33v4339jhl8k0.cloudfront.net/docs/assets/5e3f0b1e2c7d3a7e9ae777f5/images/5e7d76e82c7d3a7e9ae9e339/img-2828-1585280656-1432163705.png){width="450"
height="121"}

-   **Wirelessly:** Cozmo can connect wirelessly to each of his cubes if
    it is nearby. If a cube is too far away or the battery is low or
    dead, Cozmo may not be able to sync with it
:::

------------------------------------------------------------------------

::: sections
## Indicators of cubes not working correctly

-   One of all of the cubes LEDs do not light up but cube is detected in
    the "Settings \> Cube status" menu
-   Cozmo is unable to detect a cube
-   Cozmo loses connection to a cube
:::

------------------------------------------------------------------------

::: sections
## Troubleshooting

-   If only one cube seems to be problematic, you may switch the
    batteries of a working cube to check if this is due to a dead
    battery
-   Reset the cube:
    -   unscrew the lid from the cube
    -   wait for 10 seconds
    -   screw the lid back on
    -   the cube should flash red\>green\>blue if the battery is fine
-   Move the cubes nearer to Cozmo
-   Check the cubes are synced in "settings \> cube status"
    -   when connected, you should see a green check
    -   if Cozmo is unable to sync to a cube, it will say, \"not
        connected\"
    -   tap \"auto connect to cubes\" to re-sync the cubes, especially
        after resetting or changing the battery
:::
